Blavetee is a Nigerian custom fashion brand built around the idea that clothing should be made for you, not found by you. Every piece is crafted to order — which is both its differentiator and its challenge to communicate at scale.

Overview

A self-initiated editorial concept for Rois Beauty, a Nigerian lip product brand. Built and shot independently as a study in how product styling can carry positioning — that the way a beauty product is presented can do as much brand work as the product itself.

The Idea

Most Nigerian beauty product photography defaults to either flat e-commerce shots or busy lifestyle scenes. This concept moved in the opposite direction: editorial restraint, considered staging, a single visual idea per frame. Reveal as theatre. Indulgence as posture. Beauty as a thing you serve, not a thing you sell.

The Approach

Three frames. Each one a different posture — service, indulgence, sweetness. The visual logic was deliberately incongruent: a luxury cloche over drugstore-priced lip gloss, ostrich feathers and a silver tray, lip products buried in a stack of pancakes. The tension is the point. Beauty objects given the staging usually reserved for European houses, applied to a Nigerian indie brand.

The Outcome

A complete editorial direction the brand could use across launches, campaigns, and social — a study in what The Mused Studio's editorial direction looks like when given full creative freedom on a beauty client.

Most foreign brands get Nigeria wrong in three places.

It's not that the market doesn't want you. It's that the friction between your team in New York or London and the actual Lagos consumer is wider than it looks. Three things consistently break: logistics, creator vetting, and cultural fluency. We solve all three.

01
Logistics break.
Products held in customs. Lost in last-mile. Sent to wrong addresses by international couriers who don't understand Nigerian addressing. Campaigns delayed by weeks.
02
Creator vetting fails.
Inflated follower counts. Bot engagement. Audiences in the wrong country. Brands pay creator fees and get nothing back because the audit was done from a desk in another time zone.
03
Cultural fluency misses.
Captions that read fine in NYC but flat in Lagos. Trend timing that's two weeks behind. Tone that signals "imported" instead of "considered". Coverage that lands but doesn't convert.

Common questions

What you're probably wondering.

Do you guarantee creator posts?
No. Reputable PR doesn't promise coverage. It promises the send, the relationship management, and a transparent report. If you need guaranteed posts, you're looking at paid placement, which we can also coordinate as a separate scope at a separate fee.
How do you vet creators?
Engagement rate audits beyond surface metrics, audience demographic checks (location, age, gender split), historical brand collaboration review, and a brand-fit scoring model. We avoid creators with bot followers, inflated reach, or audience profiles that don't match yours.
What about Nigerian customs?
For most beauty, fashion, and small electronics under common value thresholds, customs is straightforward. We provide a pre-shipment guide covering declarations, HS codes, recommended carriers, and best practices. We've supported clients through complex sends and high-value shipments.
Who pays the creator fees?
You do, billed separately at cost with full transparency. We never mark up creator fees. Some Tier 1 and 2 creators in Nigeria do charge fees on top of product, particularly for paid posts. We negotiate and structure these but the fee passes through to you unchanged.
How long does a campaign take?
Pilot Send-Out: 3 to 4 weeks from brief to final report. Campaign Package: 4 to 6 weeks. Quarterly Retainer: ongoing with monthly reporting cycles. Lead time depends on shipping origin and customs.
How do payments work?
USD invoicing via Wise Business or Payoneer. 50 percent on signed scope, 50 percent on report delivery for one-off campaigns. Monthly billing for retainers, due net 14. Creator fees invoiced separately when applicable.
Can you support entry beyond influencer PR?
Yes. The Mused Studio is a full creative brand strategy practice. PR is one entry point. We also handle brand strategy, creative direction, motion content, and digital experience for international brands localising into Nigerian and African markets. Discuss in the discovery call.
