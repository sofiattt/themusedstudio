Overview

Blavetee is a Nigerian custom fashion brand built around the idea that clothing should be made for you, not found by you. Every piece is crafted to order — which is both its differentiator and its challenge to communicate at scale.

The Problem

The brand's social presence wasn't reflecting the quality of the work. Content felt inconsistent and reactive — posts that blend into a feed rather than stop it. The craftsmanship was real. The story wasn't being told.

The Approach

We started with positioning: what does Blavetee stand for beyond the product? From there, a brand voice, visual language, and content rhythm that made the custom nature of the work feel like the main event. Every piece of content built to stop the scroll and earn the follow.

The Outcome

Sustained momentum. Reach expanded well beyond existing followers and drove consistent inbound enquiries.

Market
Nigeria
Overview

Lami The Label is a Nigerian fashion brand with a distinctive design language — considered, refined, rooted in a clear aesthetic point of view. The products were strong. The brand story was still finding itself.

The Problem

The brand lacked the strategic scaffolding to communicate its value clearly. No consistent narrative tied together visual identity, messaging, and market positioning. The gap between the quality of the work and how it was being presented was costing credibility — and conversion.

The Approach

A full brand strategy engagement — defining positioning, articulating the brand's point of view, and translating that into a creative direction the team could own. Tone of voice, visual identity direction, and audience definition: who this brand is genuinely for, and who it isn't.

The Outcome

A brand that now presents with intention at every touchpoint. Clear positioning the founder can articulate in a sentence. Visual direction that makes the product quality feel obvious before the customer ever touches the garment.

Identity Direction
Positioning

Nigeria
Overview

Mimi Saleeman is a personal fashion brand built around an individual with a strong, immediately recognisable aesthetic. Personal brands carry a specific challenge: the person is the brand, but the brand needs to operate like it's bigger than the person.

The Problem

Without defined strategy, personal brands often read as lifestyle accounts rather than considered creative entities. The goal: give Mimi Saleeman the foundation to scale — from individual presence to a brand people actively seek out, reference, and return to.

The Approach

We extracted what made the brand's aesthetic genuinely distinct and built a positioning framework around it. Identity direction ensured every visual expression reinforced a coherent, ownable point of view — content with a recognisable signature.

The Outcome

A brand that operates with strategic confidence. The aesthetic that was always there now has the language and positioning to back it up — giving the founder clarity on where to show up, who to speak to, and how to grow.

Brand Strategy

Identity Direction
Market
Nigeria
Overview

Rois Beauty operates in one of Nigeria's most competitive categories. In beauty, where everyone is selling transformation, the brands that sustain are the ones with a clear, credible point of view customers trust — and return to.

The Problem

The brand was present but undifferentiated. In a market where product quality is assumed, the story around the product creates loyalty. Rois Beauty needed positioning that went beyond features and into identity.

The Approach

We built from the positioning layer upwards — defining the core brand belief, audience mindset, and the visual and verbal identity that would make Rois Beauty instantly recognisable. Every creative decision shaped by strategy.

The Outcome

A brand with a clear identity and the positioning framework to grow intentionally. Rois Beauty moved from competing on product to leading with brand — the difference between a brand customers try once and one they come back to.

The Studio

We believe brands earn trust
before they earn sales

The Mused Studio is a creative brand strategy agency founded on the belief that most brands underinvest in clarity and overspend on execution. We fix the order of operations.

Why We Exist
"The best brand work is invisible to the audience and invaluable to the business."

The Mused Studio was built from a frustration and a conviction. The frustration: watching good brands get lost not because the product was wrong, but because the story was unclear. The conviction: that strategy and aesthetics aren't separate disciplines — the best creative work is always strategically grounded.

We work with founders and brands at every stage, across every category — fashion, tech, wellness, beauty, oil & gas, lifestyle, and beyond. The difference between a brand people seek out and one they scroll past is rarely the product. It's the story.

We stay small by design. The work we do requires genuine attention. We'd rather do fewer things at a higher standard than scale in ways that dilute what we can offer.

The research
behind the strategy

Every brand strategy engagement starts with deep immersion — into the market, the competition, the cultural conversation your brand is entering. Strategy built on real research, not assumptions.

Our Point of View

Three things we believe that
shape everything we do

Positioning is the highest leverage point in brand building.

Most brand problems are positioning problems wearing a visual disguise. Before you change the logo, change what the brand stands for — then let the visuals follow from somewhere real.

Aesthetic without strategy is just expensive decoration.

A beautiful brand that doesn't communicate its value clearly is a missed opportunity. We insist on the strategic foundation first — because that's what makes the creative work actually work.

The right audience is more valuable than the biggest audience.

Growth that attracts the wrong customers is a liability. We help brands define and reach the people most likely to stay, buy again, and tell others.

What We Care About

When we work with your brand,
this is what matters

  • The brief behind the brief

    We ask more questions than most. Not to delay — because the problem a founder presents and the problem that actually needs solving are often different. We want to work on the real one.

  • Honest creative feedback

    The most valuable thing a strategic partner can offer is a perspective that isn't trying to please you — it's trying to serve the brand.

  • Work that ages well

    Trend-chasing brands date quickly. We build with a longer view — making decisions that will still be serving your brand two, three years from now.

  • The founder's vision, clarified

    We're not here to overwrite what you've built. We're here to surface what's already there and give it the strategy and expression to land the way it deserves to.
